UNIVERSITY FINANCIAL AID HANDBOOK 2024-2025

SECTION 4: TUITION PAYMENT POLICIES

4.1 Payment Deadlines
All tuition and fees are due by the first day of each semester. Students who fail to meet payment deadlines may be subject to late fees and registration holds.

4.2 Hardship Extension Policy
Students facing financial hardship may request an extension of up to 30 days for tuition payment deadlines. Requests must be submitted in writing to the Bursar's Office with documentation of hardship. Approved extensions will waive late payment fees if payment is made within the extension period.

To request a hardship extension:
1. Submit a written request to bursar@university.edu
2. Include documentation of financial hardship (medical bills, job loss, family emergency)
3. Provide student ID number and invoice number
4. Requests are reviewed within 48 hours

4.3 Late Payment Fees
Standard late payment fee is $50. However, students with approved hardship extensions are exempt from late fees if payment is made within the extension period. Late fees are applied automatically 7 days after the original due date.

4.4 Payment Plans
Students may enroll in a payment plan to spread tuition costs across the semester. Payment plans require a $50 enrollment fee and must be set up before the payment deadline. Contact the Bursar's Office for details.

SECTION 5: EMERGENCY FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE

5.1 Emergency Grant Program
Available to FGLI (First-Generation, Low-Income) students who demonstrate urgent financial need. Grants range from $200-$1,000 and are awarded within 48 hours of application submission.

Eligibility Requirements:
- Must be enrolled full-time
- Must maintain minimum 2.0 GPA
- Must demonstrate immediate financial hardship
- Must have exhausted other financial aid options

Application Process:
1. Complete Emergency Grant Application Form 10B
2. Submit to Financial Aid Office
3. Include supporting documentation
4. Applications reviewed within 24-48 hours

5.2 Hardship Fund
The University Hardship Fund provides one-time assistance to students facing unexpected financial crises. Maximum award is $500 per academic year. Applications are reviewed on a case-by-case basis.

5.3 Food Insecurity Support
Students experiencing food insecurity may access the Student Food Pantry and apply for meal plan assistance. Contact Student Support Services for more information.

SECTION 6: FINANCIAL AID APPEALS

6.1 Appeal Process
Students may appeal financial aid decisions or request reconsideration of their aid package. Appeals must be submitted within 30 days of the original decision.

6.2 Special Circumstances
Students experiencing special circumstances (job loss, medical emergency, family crisis) may request a Professional Judgment Review. This process allows the Financial Aid Office to recalculate aid eligibility based on current circumstances.

Required Documentation:
- Letter explaining special circumstances
- Supporting documentation (medical bills, termination notice, etc.)
- Updated FAFSA if applicable
- Most recent tax returns

SECTION 7: BURSAR'S OFFICE POLICIES

7.1 Payment Methods
Accepted payment methods include:
- Credit/Debit cards (online portal)
- Electronic check (ACH)
- Wire transfer
- In-person cash or check payments

7.2 Refund Policy
Students who drop courses or withdraw from the university may be eligible for tuition refunds according to the following schedule:
- 100% refund: Within first week of semester
- 50% refund: Within second week
- 25% refund: Within third week
- No refund: After third week

7.3 Registration Holds
Students with outstanding balances over $500 will have a registration hold placed on their account. Holds are removed within 24 hours of payment clearance.

BYLAW 8: STUDENT FINANCIAL RIGHTS

8.1 Right to Appeal
All students have the right to appeal financial decisions, request payment extensions, and access emergency assistance programs.

8.2 Confidentiality
All financial information submitted to the university is kept confidential and used solely for financial aid determination.

8.3 Non-Discrimination
Financial aid programs are available to all eligible students regardless of race, gender, religion, or national origin.

SECTION 9: GRANT PROGRAMS

9.1 First-Generation Student Grant
Awarded to first-generation college students demonstrating financial need. Maximum award: $2,000 per academic year. Application deadline: March 1st.

9.2 Academic Excellence Grant
Students with GPA above 3.5 may be eligible for additional grant funding. Automatic consideration for all eligible students.

9.3 Emergency Medical Grant
Available to students facing unexpected medical expenses. Maximum award: $1,500. Requires documentation from healthcare provider.
